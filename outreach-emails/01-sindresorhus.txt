Hi Sindre,

I've followed your open source work for years — your macOS CLI tools and Swift utilities (Squirrel, Plash, Devtoys, and dozens of others) are a big part of why the macOS open source ecosystem is what it is.

I'm building X-MaC, an open source macOS diagnostic and maintenance tool that combines a Rust analysis core with a SwiftUI frontend. Think of it as a developer-focused alternative to CleanMyMac — it scans for disk usage, cache bloat, PATH conflicts, broken symlinks, missing dylibs, and environment issues, then generates safe remediation scripts. It also has a GNN model (CoreML) for anomaly detection on filesystem graphs.

Your perspective on macOS developer tooling would be incredibly valuable. I'd love it if you could take a look:
